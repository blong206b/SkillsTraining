Mid/Specialized Roles: Data Engineer, Data Scientist.
Data engineers are responsible for building, maintaining, and optimizing the systems that collect, store, process, and analyze data. They play a critical role in helping organizations to make better decisions by providing them with the insights they need to understand their customers, products, and operations.

Data engineers work with a variety of technologies, including:
  • Databases: Data engineers design, build, and maintain databases to store and manage data. They also develop and maintain data pipelines to move data between different systems.
  • Big data technologies: Data engineers work with big data technologies such as Hadoop and Spark to process and analyze large datasets.
  • Cloud computing: Data engineers use cloud computing platforms such as AWS, Azure, and GCP to host and manage data systems.
Data scientists are responsible for using data to solve complex business problems. They use a variety of statistical and machine learning techniques to extract insights from data and communicate those insights to decision-makers in a way that is both clear and actionable.

Data scientists work in a variety of industries, including healthcare, finance, technology, and retail. They are employed by companies of all sizes, from startups to Fortune 500 companies.

Here are some of the specific tasks that a data scientist might perform:
  • Collect and clean data from a variety of sources
  • Explore and analyze data to identify patterns and trends
  • Build and train machine learning models to make predictions or recommendations
  • Communicate their findings to decision-makers in a clear and actionable way
